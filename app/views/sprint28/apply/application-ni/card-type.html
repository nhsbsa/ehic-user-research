Choose the cover you wish to apply for

Based on the information you have provided you may be entitled to an UK Global Health Insurance Card (GHIC) or UK European Health Insurance Card (EHIC).

What is the difference?

Both a UK GHIC and UK EHIC give you the right to access state provided healthcare cover for treatment that becomes necessary whilst temporarily staying in any country in the European Union (EU).

If you plan to temporarily stay in Norway, Iceland, Liechtenstein and Switzerland you may wish to apply for a UK EHIC as these countries are not currently covered by a UK GHIC.

In the future, more countries may be added to those accepting UK GHIC.

What countries are in the EU?
The EU countries are:

Austria, Belgium, Bulgaria, Croatia, Republic of Cyprus, Czech Republic, Denmark, Estonia, Finland, France, Germany, Greece, Hungary, Ireland, Italy, Latvia, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Malta, Netherlands, Poland, Portugal, Romania, Slovakia, Slovenia, Spain and Sweden.

Information for overseas students
If you are a student studying outside of the UK:
  • a UK EHIC will cover you for state provided healthcare cover within the EU and your country of study. You will not be able to use your card for treatment in Norway, Iceland, Liechtenstein and Switzerland except where you are studying in one of these countries.
  • a UK GHIC will cover you for both your country of study if this is in the EU, as well as any other EU country. You will not be able to use your card for treatment in Norway, Iceland, Liechtenstein and Switzerland.
