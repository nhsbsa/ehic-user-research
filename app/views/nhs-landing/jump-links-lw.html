Applying for healthcare cover abroad (GHIC and EHIC)

There are two types of cover available, use this service to apply for either a:

  • UK Global Health Insurance Card (GHIC)
  • UK European Health Insurance Card (EHIC)

Who can apply for a UK GHIC

You can apply for a UK GHIC if you:

  • live in the UK
  • normally live in the UK but are studying in the EU
  • live in the EU and have been issued with a UK S1 or A1 document
  • are an eligible family member or dependant of one of the above

Who can apply for a UK EHIC

You can apply for the new UK EHIC if you have rights under the Withdrawal Agreement. You may be able to apply if you are:

  • an EU, Swiss, Norwegian, Icelandic or Liechtenstein citizen, and you've been living in the UK since before 1 January 2021 however you may not be eligible if you are also a UK national or if you were born in the UK
  • a UK State Pensioner or receiving other exportable benefits since before 1 January 2021 and you have a registered S1 form or E121
  • a frontier worker working in one state and living in another, since before 1 January 2021 and you’re eligible for an S1 form or E106
  • a worker posted to work in another EU country, Norway, Iceland, Liechtenstein, or Switzerland by your UK employer, and you've been there since before 1 January 2021, where the country has agreed to let the posting continue
  • an eligible family member or dependant of one of the above

What can I use my card for

You can use a UK GHIC or UK EHIC to get state provided medically necessary healthcare when you’re visiting an EU country.

This includes things like:

  • emergency treatment and visits to A&E
  • treatment for a long-term, or pre-existing medical conditions
  • routine medical care for pre-existing conditions that need monitoring
  • routine maternity care, as long as you're not going abroad to give birth
  • oxygen therapy and kidney dialysis

Medically necessary healthcare means healthcare which becomes needed during your stay, and you cannot reasonably wait until you come back to UK to receive the treatment.

You’ll need to pre-arrange some treatments with the relevant healthcare provider in the country you are visiting, for example kidney dialysis or chemotherapy.

Not all state healthcare is free in the EU and you may have to pay for services that you’d get for free on the NHS.

Where can I use my card

A UK EHIC covers you whilst temporarily staying in an EU country, Norway, Iceland, Liechtenstein, and Switzerland.

A UK GHIC only covers you whilst temporarily staying in an EU country, it doesn’t cover you while staying in Norway, Iceland, Liechtenstein, or Switzerland.

The EU countries are:

Austria, Belgium, Bulgaria, Croatia, Republic of Cyprus, Czech Republic, Denmark, Estonia, Finland, France, Germany, Greece, Hungary, Ireland, Italy, Latvia, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Malta, Netherlands, Poland, Portugal, Romania, Slovakia, Slovenia, Spain, and Sweden.

You may be asked to provide further evidence to support your application. Applying takes around 5 minutes.

A UK GHIC or UK EHIC is free of charge. Beware of unofficial websites, they may charge you a fee to apply.

It’s currently taking longer than usual to process new UK EHICs due to high demand. We’re working to resolve this and will process all applications as soon as possible.

Arrange travel insurance

Your UK EHIC or UK GHIC card is not a substitute for travel insurance. It may not cover all health costs and never covers repatriation costs. Make sure you have travel insurance as well as an UK EHIC or UK GHIC.

If you are abroad and do not have your card with you

You can get a Provisional Replacement Certificate (PRC) to prove your entitlement to medically necessary healthcare if you travel to Europe without your UK GHIC or UK EHIC and need treatment during your visit.

If you need it outside opening hours, you should call as soon as possible the next working day.

The PRC will give you the same cover as an UK EHIC or UK GHIC until you return home.

When calling for a PRC, you'll need to give:

  • your National Insurance number
  • your name
  • your address
  • your date of birth
  • the name of the treatment facility
  • the email address for the specific department of the organisation providing your treatment

To apply for a PRC contact the Overseas Healthcare Services.

Someone else can apply for a PRC on your behalf.

Claiming a refund

Depending on the country you visit you may be expected to pay all or part of your bill upfront and then claim a refund afterwards.

Some countries ask patients to pay a contribution towards the cost of their care, such as for prescription costs. This is known as a co-payment or patient share.

You can claim back the difference between the total bill and the patient share, but the actual patient share is not refundable.

Keep all receipts and any paperwork, you or your insurance company may need them if you're applying for a refund.
