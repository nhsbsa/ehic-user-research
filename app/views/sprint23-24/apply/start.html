Get healthcare cover for travelling abroad

Use this service to find out which card you are entitled to and apply for either:

  • UK Global Health Insurance Card (GHIC)
  • UK European Health Insurance Card (EHIC)

If you're a UK resident you’re entitled to a UK GHIC and may also be entitled to a new UK EHIC

Where are the cards valid

Both cards cover you for necessary state provided healthcare treatment whilst temporarily staying in an EU country.

The EU countries are:

Austria, Belgium, Bulgaria, Croatia, Republic of Cyprus, Czech Republic, Denmark, Estonia, Finland, France, Germany, Greece, Hungary, Ireland, Italy, Latvia, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Malta, Netherlands, Poland, Portugal, Romania, Slovakia, Slovenia, Spain and Sweden.


This cover will give you access to state provided healthcare while staying in an EU country for things like:

  • emergency treatment
  • visits to A&E
  • routine maternity care, unless you're going abroad to give birth

If you have an European Health Insurance Card (EHIC)

Your EHIC will be valid until the expiry date on the card. You can apply for a new card up to 6 months before its expiry date.

Once it expires, you’ll need to apply for a new card.

Information:

Applying takes around 5 minutes.

Arrange travel insurance

Your UK EHIC or UK GHIC card is not a substitute for travel insurance. It may not cover all health costs and never covers repatriation costs. Make sure you have travel insurance as well as an UK EHIC or UK GHIC.
