Co-payments or patient contributions

Not all state healthcare is free in other countries.

In some countries, a resident of that country may have to pay towards the cost of their treatment. This is known as a co-payment or a patient contribution. This means that you may need to pay for services that you would get for free on the NHS.

These payments are typically for services like GP or dentist consultations, prescriptions, or hospital stays.

Check the Foreign Office country guides on GOV.UK for information on how to access treatment in the country you're visiting and what may be required.

You cannot apply for a refund for a co-payment. You can only claim a refund for costs which would have been covered by a UK European Health Insurance Card (EHIC), UK Global Health Insurance Card (GHIC), or Provisional Replacement Certificate (PRC).
