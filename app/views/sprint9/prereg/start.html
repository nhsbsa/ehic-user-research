Get healthcare cover for visiting the EU, EEA or Switzerland (EHIC)

Thank you for taking part in the pre-registration process for a new European Health Insurance Card (EHIC).

EHIC from 1 January 2021

The UK left the European Union (EU) on 31 January 2020 and is now in a transition period until the end of 31 December 2020.

From 1 January 2021, anyone holding a registered UK-issued S1 certificate who lives in another EEA country or Switzerland will continue to access healthcare in the same way they currently do. They will also be entitled to an EHIC.

The EHIC will cover you for all state-funded, medically necessary and needs-arising healthcare when visiting another EEA country or Switzerland. (Except the country you are residing in or the UK).

The pre-registration process

By pre-registering, you will be able to apply for an EHIC as an S1 holder before 1 January 2021. This will ensure you have your EHIC ready for the end of the transition period.

During the pre-registration process, we will ask you a series of questions. Enter your relevant details into the Overseas Healthcare Services online portal.

We will tell you during the pre-registration process if you do not meet the eligibility criteria for an EHIC.

If your application is successful, we will send you confirmation to the email address you give us in your application. This email will contain further information, including when you will receive your new EHIC.

For information on how we use your data, please refer to our privacy notice.
